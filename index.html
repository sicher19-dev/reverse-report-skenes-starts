The Macro Read

The line lies

A 3-earned-run line off 4.2 innings reads like a beating. It wasn’t. Skenes generated weak contact and whiffs all night — his run-value impact was +0.20, basically league-average neutral, not the −1.5 a true blow-up looks like. What actually happened: the defense gave nothing back, the scorer charged two gift hits, and the pitch count ballooned the second and third time through until he was pulled mid-5th.

Recurring Tendencies

What turned a quality start into a loss

3 plays

The Gonzales factor at third

Three plays at 3B went against him: the leadoff “double” (89.9-mph liner deflected off the glove), a bunt single he couldn’t field cleanly, and a throwing error. Two were ruled hits and counted as earned. That’s a leadoff runner in scoring position and two extra baserunners that were never really earned.

+0.20 RV

The box score vs. the batted ball

3 ER, but a neutral +0.20 run value and a parade of soft outs. Trace the runs: one set up by the bunt hit, one by an 8-pitch walk, and only one on clean back-to-back 100-mph contact. By quality, this was a 1-run start.

6.8 P/PA

The third-time-through wall

TTO1 was 3.0 pitches per batter. By TTO3 it was 6.8. The Astros stopped chasing, fouled off the velocity, and the hard contact (105, 100, 102 mph) all came in that final inning — on a fastball-heavy plan they’d now seen twice.

4 × 8-pitch

Four eight-pitch at-bats

PA10, 13, 17 and 22 each went eight pitches — 32 pitches on four hitters. Two were walks/hits, two were strikeouts, but all four were foul-off marathons or over-mixed sequences (four changeups to Paredes in one AB). That’s the inning or two of length he left on the table.

The Bottom Line

What the line should have been

Convert the two gift hits into the outs they should’ve been and the night reshapes completely: no leadoff runner in scoring position in the 1st, no leadoff baserunner in the 3rd, fewer pitches, and a run ledger that reads 1 instead of 3.

Keep

Almost everything. The stuff played — 7 K, 27.5% CSW, soft contact across the board, and 10 clean quick outs. The first-time-through arsenal was 3.0 pitches per hitter. There is no “fix the pitcher” story here.

Fix

Only the efficiency. End the foul-off at-bats sooner (don’t throw the changeup four times to a contact hitter; don’t run five straight four-seamers), and vary the look the third time through so the velocity isn’t the whole plan. That’s the difference between 4.2 and 6+ innings.
