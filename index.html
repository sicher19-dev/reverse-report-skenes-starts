The Bottom Line

A quality start, the hard way

Six innings, two earned, seven punchouts against the deepest lineup in baseball — with the underlying contact telling you he wasn’t fooling them the way he fooled Philadelphia. That makes the result more impressive, not less: he survived a barrage on guile and the strikeout. The Dodgers went on to win 12–3 — the bulk of it after he exited — which only underlines that his two runs were the night’s lone act of run prevention.

Keep

The competitiveness with two on. Every scoring threat met a strikeout or a double-play grounder. The TTO2 sequencing — mixing the soft and spin once the lefties showed they were on the heater — is the model for facing this lineup again.

Watch

The four-seam exposure to the left-handed bats, and the two walks. Muncy’s 3-for-3 and the 105/105/107 doubles all came against velocity in the zone. Next time vs LA: more spin/soft to the lefties early, and don’t walk the bottom of the order.
