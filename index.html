The Bottom Line

A 4-ER line that pitched like a good one

The box score will remember four earned runs; the underlying night was a net-positive outing with seven strikeouts and four dominant innings around one frame that snowballed on soft contact and two walks. There’s no stuff problem and no command spiral to fix — just a single inning to keep from compounding.

Keep

The stuff and the bounce-back. A 29.2% CSW, seven punchouts, the side struck out in the 4th, and a 1-2-3 fifth after a four-run frame — that resilience is the headline, not the line. The changeup and four-seam were missing bats all night.

Fix

Don’t let a soft-contact inning compound. The two walks turned a couple of seeing-eye singles into a bases-loaded, no-out crisis. Limiting the free passes in a cluster inning is the difference between a one-run frame and a four-run one — the stuff was good enough to escape if the bases stay manageable.
