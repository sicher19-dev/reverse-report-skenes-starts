The Bottom Line

What 9-on-80 looks like

The blueprint is already in this game. PA3 (Busch, 4-pitch K) and PA16 (Ballesteros, 1-pitch pop-out) are the template: get the eye-level read, then one decisive put-away. Stack that profile and the math changes fast.

Keep

The stuff is not the problem — 18 whiffs, 10 K, one earned run. The 4-seam-up / change-under combo to lefties and the FF/sweeper/slider ladder to righties the first time through are elite. Don’t touch them.

Fix

Steal first-pitch strikes (61% → 65%+), put hitters away in the zone they’re already covering instead of hunting the perfect whiff, and rotate the put-away pitch by trip so PCA and Happ never see the same look three times.

THE TAX, ITEMIZED:
• 3 strikeout PAs at 6–7 pitches (PA1, 6, 8, 17) → trim to ~4 each → ~7 pitches saved
• 2 sweeper-led walks (PA9, 12) → first-pitch strikes → ~6 pitches + 2 baserunners saved
• Net: a ~4.48 → ~3.9 P/PA shift ≈ 13 fewer pitches over 23 batters
→ 103 pitches becomes ~90 → that’s the 7th, and a look at the 8th.
