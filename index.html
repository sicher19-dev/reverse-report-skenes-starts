The Bottom Line

He won the start; the park won the game

Strip out the altitude and the defense and this is a two-hit, eight-strikeout outing. Coors and a misplay in center turned a line drive into an inside-the-park homer, and the park turned a soft grounder into a double; the offense managed a single run behind him. The process to act on is small and specific: command in to left-handed hitters.

Keep

The plan and the put-away. Eight K’s and a 27.9% CSW at altitude is excellent, the four-seam/changeup approach to a lefty lineup was right, and escaping the 4th-inning jam with a double play is exactly the resilience you want on a tough-park night.

Fix

Command to the inner third — that’s the whole list. The four free runners (two walks and two hit batters) were all misses in to left-handed bats. There was no true mistake pitch: the leadoff run came on a misplayed line drive, not a hung ball. Tighten the command in and the free runners disappear.
