The Fix  // velocity & spin protocol

Why the fastball is fading — and the plan to get it back

What we know from the data: spin is down on every pitch, velo fades within games and across starts, spin-per-mph on the four-seam fell from 22.7 to 20.6 — while extension, ride, and sinker run all held. The release is intact. The engine is running low.

The likely causes, ranked by evidence

1
Accumulated fatigue / dead-arm phase

The strongest fit: a monotonic six-start slide, worse late in games, hitting every pitch equally, with the release intact. Seventeen starts at max-effort stuff on a career-high innings pace, entering July. This is the classic signature.

2
Fingertip force / grip component

Spin fell more than velo alone explains — the spin-per-mph ratio dropped. That points at finger pressure at release: check nails, blisters, callus wear, and grip in July humidity. Cheap to rule out; ask him directly.

3
Rotational-timing decay

Trunk/pelvis velocity or hip-shoulder timing can erode under fatigue while the release point stays clean — exactly this pattern. Not guessable from pitch data; this is what the mocap bullpen is for.

×
What it is NOT

Not a slot change (ride held at 13.2″), not stride or release depth (extension 6.85′), not one pitch’s grip (systemic). Don’t let anyone chase a mechanical rebuild off this.

The protocol

1
Mocap bullpen this week — the decision gate

Theia + TrackMan, compared against the April baseline: arm speed, pelvis/trunk angular velocity, timing gaps. If arm speed is down → recovery problem. If arm speed holds but spin doesn’t → grip problem. Derek owns this; it splits the whole tree.

2
Recovery block into the All-Star break

The break is 1–2 starts away — the natural reset. Push for the extra rest day, cut between-start bullpen volume in half (shape work moves to flat ground), soft-cap the next start near 85 pitches. Re-baseline everything after the break.

3
Track spin-per-mph daily

Every bullpen pitch on TrackMan. The four-seam ratio is the tripwire: 22.7 season, 20.6 tonight. A recovering ratio is a recovering arm — and it’ll show in the pen before it shows in a box score.

4
Low-spin identity until it’s back

Lean the pitches that survive spin loss: the sinker (seam-driven — its run held at 17.4″ tonight) and the changeup (40% whiff). Park the sweeper (needs spin to sweep) and the splitter (no feel) — east-west with the two-seam and change, four-seam up used selectively, not as the primary.

STANDING RULE — nothing counts as fixed until it shows in game at-bats. The first 15 pitches of the next start are the test: four-seam spin-per-mph and sweeper break vs tonight’s floor.
